Key Takeaways
  1. 1IMM quarterly report finds winter 2025/6 costs hit $6.53B (+45% YoY), the highest since 2014, with 45% of seasonal costs concentrated in a 19-day cold snap. DA A/S adding an estimated $574M (10.1%) in incremental costs.
  2. 2CAR-SA: tariff redlines now in process. Proposes to sunset the entire Claimed Capability Audit regime on August 31, 2027, replacing it with rolling 36-month Global/Seasonal Maximum Capability constructs.
  3. 3A new complete forced outage across a Capacity Commitment Period now zeroes out a resource's Seasonal MRI Capacity Value in cases of late-date catastrophic outages.
  4. 4ISO backtracks and proposes to maintain the Balancing Ratio cap at 1, consistent with FERC's Jan 2026 NEPGA order.
  5. 5Import qualification conformed with a 16-hour fuel delivery demonstration (down from 24) and a winter firm gas contract requirement.
Executive Summary

The July 21, 2026 Markets Committee meeting delivered both a striking Winter 2026 market retrospective and the next major step in the CAR-SA seasonal accreditation package — advancing tariff redlines for auditing and qualification, and resolving a substantial set of stakeholder follow-up questions ahead of the targeted Q4 2026 Technical Committee and PC votes.

Key Developments

  • CAR-SA moves from concept to codified tariff language. ISO has put initial redlines out for the two central rulebooks, with a hard sunset date (August 31, 2027) for all existing audit types.
  • A new zero-value provision for full-CCP forced outages creates a material settlement exposure with no current-tariff analog.
  • Winter 2026 was a stress test of the redesigned energy market. Total wholesale cost hit $6.53B (+45% YoY, highest since 2014); the IMM attributes ~$574M (10.1%) of incremental cost to the DA A/S design.

Near-Term Deadlines & Process Milestones

  • July 27, 2026 — Stakeholder questions on CAR-SA redlines due to ISO-NE.
  • Q4 2026 — CAR-SA Technical Committee and PC votes; FERC filing to follow; audit sunset August 31, 2027.

The IMM's Winter 2026 QMR documents the most consequential winter market outcome since 2014. Total wholesale cost reached $6.53 billion ($196/MWh), up 45% YoY, of which energy was $6.08 billion (93%) and capacity $266 million (4%). All-in day-ahead price including FERP averaged $165.49/MWh — the highest since Standard Market Design began.

The season's severity was driven by weather. Average temperature 26°F (-6.8°F vs. normal), a 19-day cold snap from January 23, and January HDDs of 1,274 (highest since 2015). Peak load hit 20,221 MW on January 25 — highest since 2019.

Key Figure
The IMM's simulation attributes $574M (10.1%) of incremental cost to the DA A/S design vs. an energy-only market, with January 25–29 accounting for two-thirds of that increment.

Oil supplied 6% of energy — highest since at least 2015 — and averaged ~4,000 MW/hour from Jan 24 through Feb 9. Stored-fuel opportunity-costs activated on 53 days across 88 assets, with day-ahead OCs reaching $460/MWh.
