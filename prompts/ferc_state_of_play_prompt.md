report on ONE FERC docket for a portfolio strategist who has NOT followed
it filing-by-filing and wants a single document that tells the whole
story: what was filed, who lined up where, what the Commission has done,
and what happens next.

[CONTEXT]
The [DOCKET] block identifies the proceeding. [INTERVENORS] lists every
party that moved to intervene, chronologically. [PROCEDURAL TIMELINE] is
the complete filing record — including notices and procedural items that
carry no summary. [PENDING DEADLINES] lists open comment/response dates.
[FILING SUMMARIES — OLDEST FIRST] contains the internal summary of every
substantive filing, delimited by `=== FILING n of N — ... ===` headers
carrying date, class/type, party, accession number, and FERC cite where
assigned.

When a [PRIOR STATE OF PLAY] block is present, it is the previous version
of this report and may contain manual analyst edits — corrections,
added framing, emphasis. Treat those edits as authoritative analyst
judgment: preserve them wherever they remain accurate, update them where
new filings supersede them, and never silently drop them.

[FORMAT INSTRUCTIONS]
Produce the report in exactly this structure. Heading levels are
load-bearing for downstream rendering: sections are `## `, never deeper
except where noted. Do not add an H1 title. Do not include images. If the
docket is young (one or two filings), keep every section but compress —
never pad.

---

## Key Takeaways

At most 5 bullets, ranked by impact. Each is one sentence of at most 25
words a reader could act on without reading further.

---

## The Proceeding

One tight paragraph (~80–120 words): what was filed, by whom, under what
authority, what it would change, and the requested effective date.

---

## Where Parties Stand

The alignment map. Group parties by stance (support / support with
conditions / opposed / procedural-only), with each group's core argument
in one or two sentences. Note intervenors who have taken no substantive
position. Use a short table if it reads better than prose.

---

## Commission Action

What FERC (or an ALJ) has done so far, with cites and dates: orders
issued, conditions imposed, compliance directives and their deadlines.
If the Commission has not yet acted, state the statutory action date if
inferable and say "No Commission action yet."

---

## The Story So Far

The narrative core, ~250–450 words in chronological arcs (use short `### `
subheads only if there are distinct phases). Trace the docket: the filing,
the intervention wave, where comments concentrated their fire, how the
filer responded, what the Commission did. Name the inflection points and
attribute positions to parties where the record supports it. Write it as
one story, not a filing-by-filing recap.

---

## Open Items & What Comes Next

Short bullets: pending deadlines (comments, answers, compliance filings),
rehearing windows, expected next filings, linked proceedings, and the
single thing to watch that most determines the outcome. If nothing is
pending, say so and state what would restart movement.
