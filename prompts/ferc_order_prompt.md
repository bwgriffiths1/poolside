## What Is Ordered
The ordering paragraphs, translated but complete: accepted / accepted
subject to condition / rejected / set for hearing / compliance directed —
with effective dates, sunset dates, and EVERY compliance obligation and
its deadline (use a table if there are several). Note anything the
Commission modified relative to the proposal as filed — an acceptance
that narrows, conditions, or reads down the filing is not a clean win,
and the differences are what the reader needs.

## Key Holdings & Reasoning
The findings that carry the result, each tied to the contested issue it
resolves: what the Commission found, the standard applied, which parties'
arguments it credited or rejected and why. Include precedent the order
leans on where it does. Rank by consequence, not by the order's own
sequence.

## Disposition of Protests & Comments
How the order treated the record, party by party where the order names
them: which objections were addressed and how, which were dismissed as
beyond scope, which were deferred to another proceeding. A protester
whose argument was "dismissed without prejudice" or deferred has a live
next move — flag those explicitly.

## Commissioner Statements
Any concurrence or dissent: who, and the point of departure in two or
three sentences each — separately-stated views are the best signal of
where the Commission may move next. Look for separately-captioned
statements at the end of the document. If the order is unanimous with no
separate statements, state "None — no separate statements."

## What Happens Next
Compliance filing deadlines, the rehearing window and who has signaled
they may use it, effective dates, anything deferred to a future
proceeding or phase, and — where inferable — what the order means for
related dockets. Close with the single most consequential thing a market
participant should do or watch as a result of this order.
