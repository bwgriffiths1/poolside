## Key Takeaways

- IMM quarterly report finds winter 2025/6 costs hit **$6.53B (+45% YoY)**, the highest since 2014, with 45% of seasonal costs concentrated in a 19-day cold snap. DA A/S adding an estimated $574M (10.1%) in incremental costs.
- CAR-SA: tariff redlines now in process. Proposes to sunset the entire Claimed Capability Audit regime on August 31, 2027, replacing it with rolling 36-month Global/Seasonal Maximum Capability constructs.
- A new complete forced outage across a Capacity Commitment Period now zeroes out a resource's Seasonal MRI Capacity Value in cases of late-date catastrophic outages.
- ISO backtracks and proposes to maintain the Balancing Ratio cap at 1, consistent with FERC's Jan 2026 NEPGA order.
- Import qualification conformed with a 16-hour fuel delivery demonstration (down from 24) and a winter firm gas contract requirement.

## 2 — Winter Quarterly Markets Report

The IMM's Winter 2026 QMR documents the most consequential winter market outcome since 2014. Total wholesale cost reached **$6.53 billion ($196/MWh)**, up 45% YoY, of which energy was **$6.08 billion (93%)** and capacity **$266 million (4%)**. All-in day-ahead price including FERP averaged **$165.49/MWh — the highest since Standard Market Design began**.

| Metric | Winter 2026 | YoY |
|---|---|---|
| Total wholesale cost | $6.53B | +45% |
| Energy (incl. FER credits) | $6.08B | +48% |
| Capacity | $266M | -3% |
| Day-ahead Hub LMP | $147.99 | +27% |
| All-in DA price (w/ FERP) | $165.49 | +29% |

**The season's severity was driven by weather.** Average temperature 26°F (-6.8°F vs. normal), a 19-day cold snap from January 23, and January HDDs of 1,274 (highest since 2015). Peak load hit 20,221 MW on January 25 — highest since 2019.

> [!Key Figure] The IMM's simulation attributes **$574M (10.1%)** of incremental cost to the DA A/S design vs. an energy-only market, with January 25–29 accounting for two-thirds of that increment.

Oil supplied **6% of energy — highest since at least 2015** — and averaged ~4,000 MW/hour from Jan 24 through Feb 9. Stored-fuel opportunity-costs activated on 53 days across 88 assets, with day-ahead OCs reaching $460/MWh.

### 2.1 — DA A/S Incremental Cost Estimate

The IMM's simulation-based analysis estimates that the DA A/S market added **$574 million (10.1%)** in total day-ahead-plus-deviation-settlement costs vs. the prior energy-only design, with the five-day period January 25–29 accounting for **$389 million (over two-thirds)**.
